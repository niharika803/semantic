Ramappa Temple 🛕

An architectural marvel from the Kakatiya dynasty

Introduction

The Ramappa Temple, also known as the Ramalingeswara temple, is a stunning example of Kakatiya dynasty architecture. Located in the Palampet village of Telangana, India, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and stands as a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Details about the Place

Built in the 13th century, the Ramappa Temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and stellar architecture. The temple features a star-shaped platform and beautifully carved pillars, each telling a story of its own. The main deity, Lord Shiva, is enshrined in the form of a Shivalinga.

  • The roof (garbhalayam) of the temple is built with bricks, which are so light that they can float on water.
  • The samples of floating bricks from Bidar are similar to those from Warangal as far as the method of manufacture is concerned.

Why I Love Ramappa Temple

  • I love the Ramappa Temple because it is not just a place of worship, but also a piece of history frozen in time.
  • The exquisite carvings, the serene atmosphere, and the historical significance make it a place worth visiting.
  • It's a perfect blend of spirituality and architectural brilliance. Moreover, the temple's inclusion in the UNESCO World Heritage Site list in 2021 adds to its global recognition and prestige.
